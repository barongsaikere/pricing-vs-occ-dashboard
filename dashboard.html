Occupancy-First Pricing Insights

Every Empty Room = Lost Total Revenue

An empty room doesn't just lose room revenue — it loses restaurant, spa, minibar, activities, and all ancillary revenue that a guest brings. With current OCC at 70.39% (Mar) and 55.5% (Apr), every pricing decision should ask: "Will this help fill more rooms?" More guests = more total property revenue.

Reduce Overpriced Dates Immediately

Dates where website is >3% above cheapest competitor are flagged as REDUCE. These are dates where potential guests see a cheaper option elsewhere and book there — or don't book at all. Match or beat market price to capture every possible booking. The ancillary revenue from each guest more than compensates for a small rate reduction.

Competitive Dates = Push Marketing

Dates where website is already at or below market price are your competitive advantage. Price is right — now push these dates through marketing, promotions, email campaigns, and OTA visibility boosts. The goal is volume: fill rooms first, upsell F&B and spa on arrival.

Booking Window Context

Future months naturally show lower OCC — bookings accelerate inside 30 days. But in a challenging market, don't wait for last-minute demand that may not come. Be proactive: ensure competitive pricing across all lead times. Better to lock in bookings early at market rate than gamble on late demand.

Rate Parity = Occupancy Leak

Tripadvisor (64 dates), Agoda (58 dates), klook (42 dates) are pricing below your website. This means potential direct bookings are leaking to OTAs — or worse, guests choose a competitor. Fix rate parity urgently: every violation is a potential lost guest, not just a lost direct booking.
